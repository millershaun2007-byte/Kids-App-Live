üíú About Me & Why I Built This App

üåü Hi, I'm Shaun

I'm 43 years old. I've worked in crisis support for 6 years - as a crisis supporter, call coach, mentor, and shift supervisor. I have a Diploma of Community Services. But for most of my life, I navigated the world with undiagnosed ADHD - not understanding why things felt so hard, why I couldn't focus like others, why the world felt overwhelming.

Then I had kids. My daughter Mia is six years old. She has ADHD, ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder), and a PDA (Pathological Demand Avoidance) profile. She's also the most creative, imaginative, brilliant kid I've ever met.

When I watch her, I see myself. The struggles. The meltdowns. But I also see something the world often misses: the incredible creativity, the unique way she sees things, the talents that neurotypical kids don't have.

I thought my years in mental health crisis support had taught me about neurodivergence. But lived experience - both my own and watching Mia - taught me more than any training ever could.

üö´ Why Traditional Apps & Schools Fail Kids Like Mia

The school system isn't designed for neurodivergent kids. The educational apps out there? Same story. I tried everything - expensive platforms, "highly rated" apps, resources recommended by teachers. But here's what I found:

  • Too much sensory overload - flashing colors, loud sounds, chaotic interfaces that trigger meltdowns
  • No understanding of PDA - direct demands create instant resistance and shutdown
  • Rigid, inflexible paths - if Mia needed a break, there was nowhere safe to go
  • Zero accommodations for processing differences - everything moved too fast with unclear instructions
  • Punishing "wrong" answers - big red X marks and harsh sounds that destroy confidence
  • Constant ads and interruptions - breaking focus and adding more demands

Watching Mia struggle with these apps - watching her shut down, resist, and feel like she was "failing" - broke my heart.

üí° So I Built What Mia Needed

This app was born out of necessity, love, and frustration with a world that doesn't understand kids like Mia. I wanted to create something that:

  • ‚úÖ Respects sensory needs - calm colors, adjustable settings, a dedicated Calm Corner for regulation
  • ‚úÖ Gives genuine choice - no forced demands (PDA-friendly), she picks what she wants to do
  • ‚úÖ Celebrates her interests - unicorns, princesses, dragons, dinosaurs, creativity!
  • ‚úÖ Builds confidence - positive reinforcement, gentle encouragement, no punishment
  • ‚úÖ Provides safe breaks - when she's overwhelmed, the Calm Corner is always there
  • ‚úÖ Is 100% safe - no data collection, no ads, no hidden agendas, no exploitation

And honestly? I also wanted to give myself and other parents a break. If Mia can use this app for even 30 minutes, that's 30 minutes where she's learning, I'm not in crisis mode, and we both get to breathe.

‚ú® The Creativity & Talent Neurodivergent Kids Have is Incredible

Here's what people don't talk about enough: Neurodivergent kids are amazingly creative and talented. Mia's imagination is off the charts. The stories she tells, the creatures she invents, the connections she makes that I'd never think of - it's breathtaking.

Kids with ADHD hyperfocus on what they love. Autistic kids see details and patterns others miss. Kids with PDA are incredible problem-solvers when given autonomy. These aren't deficits - they're different kinds of brilliance.

But the world keeps trying to "fix" them instead of celebrating what makes them extraordinary. This app is designed to let their creativity shine, not be crushed by rigid expectations.

üåà Everyone Deserves Respect, Love, Care & The Right to Learn

I wanted the world to understand the day-to-day challenges that kids like Mia face. But more than that, I wanted to create a space where they can learn without judgment, without pressure, without feeling broken.

Every child - whether they have ADHD, autism, PDA, dyslexia, sensory processing differences, anxiety, or just learn differently - deserves the same respect, the same love, the same care, and the same rights to learn as any other child.

This app was made with Mia in mind. Tested with her. Improved because of her feedback. Shaped by her needs and her creativity. Not as an afterthought - as the entire foundation.

üíú We're In This Together

This is still a work in progress, just like parenting. I'm constantly improving it based on what works for Mia - and what works for your kids too.

Thank you for trusting me with your child's learning journey. Thank you for understanding that our kids deserve better. Thank you for being part of this community.

With love, understanding, and solidarity,

Why I Built This App (And Why Mia Needed It)

December 7, 2025
üè∑Ô∏è ADHD | Autism | PDA Profile | Parent Advocacy

I'm Shaun. I'm 43. I work in crisis support. And I spent most of my life not knowing I had ADHD.

For 6 years, I've been a crisis supporter, call coach, mentor, and shift supervisor helping people through their hardest moments. I have a Diploma of Community Services. I thought I understood mental health and neurodivergence.

But for decades before my diagnosis, I didn't understand why everything felt harder for me. Why I couldn't focus like other people. Why I'd get overwhelmed so easily. Why the world just felt... too much.

Then I had kids. And my daughter Mia - beautiful, brilliant, creative, six-year-old Mia - was diagnosed with ADHD, ASD, and has a PDA profile.

üí° Suddenly, everything made sense. I saw myself in her. But I also saw something incredible: her imagination, her creativity, her unique way of seeing the world that neurotypical kids don't have. She's not broken. She's extraordinary.

üåü Neurodivergent Kids Are Creative Geniuses (And the World Misses It)

Mia can hyperfocus on creating stories for hours. She sees patterns and details I'd never notice. She invents entire worlds in her head - dragons with personalities, unicorns with backstories, adventures that rival anything I've read in books.

This is what ADHD and autism look like when you're not trying to "fix" kids. When you give them space to be creative. When you let them explore what they love. When you stop demanding and start inviting.

‚ú® These kids aren't deficits waiting to be corrected. They're different kinds of brilliance waiting to be celebrated.

üö´ But Schools & Apps Don't See It That Way

I watch Mia navigate a school system that wasn't designed for her. I watch her use educational apps that trigger meltdowns. The flashing screens. The timers counting down. The big red X marks when she gets something "wrong." The constant demands.

For kids with PDA (Pathological Demand Avoidance), every direct instruction feels like pressure. Every "you have to do this" creates resistance. She's not being difficult - she's protecting herself from overwhelm.

But the educational apps out there? They don't get it. They demand. They pressure. They punish. They overstimulate. And then parents are told their kid is the problem.

üé® So I Built a Space Where Creativity Can Flourish

I wanted Mia to have an app where she could learn AND create. Where her imagination could run wild. Where she had:

  • Freedom to choose - No forced paths. She picks what interests her.
  • Creative outlets - Design creatures, write stories, make art.
  • A safe space to regulate - The Calm Corner for when she's overwhelmed.
  • Encouragement, not punishment - Gentle feedback that builds confidence.
  • Time to think - No timers. No rush. Process at her pace.
  • Sensory control - Adjustable colors, sounds, animations to match her needs.

This isn't about "fixing" neurodivergent kids. It's about giving them a space where their creativity, their unique thinking, and their amazing talents can shine.

üíú And honestly? I also wanted to give myself - and other parents - a break. If Mia can use this app for 30 minutes, that's 30 minutes where we're both okay. Where she's learning. Where I'm not in crisis mode. Where we both get to breathe.

üíú This is About More Than an App - It's About Changing How We See These Kids

I built this because I wanted the world to see what I see in Mia: brilliance, creativity, talent, and potential. Not a list of deficits. Not a kid who needs to be "fixed." A kid who deserves respect, love, and the right to learn in a way that works for her brain.

This app is still a work in progress. There are bugs to fix. Features to add. But you know what? Mia uses it. She asks to use it. And when she's using it, she's creating. She's learning. She's thriving.

üåà If your child has that spark of creativity, that unique way of seeing things, that amazing talent the world doesn't always appreciate - this app was built for them.

Your child isn't broken. They're extraordinary. Let's celebrate that together. üåü
